Who We Are

Glass fabricators.
Glaziers by trade.
GTA by choice.

Toronto Premium Glass was founded by journeyman glaziers who grew up on job sites — not in showrooms. We understand glass from the inside out: how it's cut, how it fails, what makes a seal last, and why no two installations are ever truly alike.

Over the past decade, we've completed more than 500 projects across the Greater Toronto Area — from single-bathroom shower enclosures in North York to full commercial glass wall systems in Mississauga. Our reputation is built entirely on referrals, which tells you something about how we work.

We're not a big-box subcontractor. We don't send crews who've never held a suction cup. Every measurement, every cut, every install is handled by glaziers who take personal pride in their work — because this is their trade, not just a job.

"Glass is one of the most demanding materials in interior design. Get it right and it disappears — the space becomes everything. Get it wrong and it never lets you forget."

We've installed glass in some of the most architecturally demanding homes and commercial spaces in the GTA. The standard we hold ourselves to isn't set by the industry — it's set by the work itself.

What Sets Us Apart

Three pillars.
One standard.

01 · Precision
Measured
to the
millimetre

We measure everything twice and cut once. Our glaziers use calibrated digital tools on every site visit and cross-check every dimension before glass is ordered. The result is an install that fits perfectly — always.

02 · Materials
Premium
glass, always

We source from certified fabricators and use only tempered, annealed, or laminated glass that meets Canadian safety standards. No substitutions, no cheap hardware. Your project deserves materials that last decades.

03 · Trade Expertise
Glaziers
not
labourers

There's a meaningful difference between someone who installs glass and someone who understands it. Our crews come from the glazing trade — they read plans, solve problems on-site, and know when to flag an issue before it becomes a defect.
